Multiple Linear Regression

By using this method, we want to see how wins correlate to 19 statistical variables for each team. From those results, create and train a model to predict wins.

After assembling the dataset, I used plotly and scikit-learn to plot team wins vs. team stats. After the model was scaled and fitted, then trained to the training data, I calculated the scores for the training and testing data:

Training Score: 0.10250841407489408

Testing Score: -0.03936315633854104

These scores indicate a very weak correlation between team wins to team stats, and the model would poorly predict team wins. The above residual plot also indicates this as well.

Another attempt was made to look at a different component to better predict teams win. I used plotly and scikit-learn to plot team win-loss % vs. team stats (19 statistical variables). After the model was scaled and fitted, then trained to the training data, I calculated the scores for the training and testing data:

Training Score: 0.8834962024251878

Testing Score: 0.8923338300921946

In comparison to the results above, these scores indicate a strong correlation between team win-loss % to team stats, and the model would improve tremendously in predicting team win-loss percentages. The above residual plot also indicates this as well.
